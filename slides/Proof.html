Proof

\(\Delta M_{\rm ext}\approx -T\Delta S \)


[Goon & Penco, 1909.05254]

Focus: \(\Delta M \) and \(\Delta S \)

States characterized by \(M,S\) and other parameters \(\Qvec\)

Keep \(\Qvec\) fixed in all comparisons

Question 1: Take a state \( (M,\Qvec)\) which exists in both theories.

How does the entropy of state \( (M,\Qvec)\) change? \(\Delta S\)

Question 2: Fix \(\Qvec\) and study minimum energy, \(T\longrightarrow 0\) regime.

How does this minimum energy change? \(\Delta M_{\rm ext}\)

Will show \(\Delta M_{\rm ext}\propto -\Delta S\)

Heuristic Argument for \(\Delta M_{\rm ext}\propto -\Delta S\)

Technical steps straightforward, but first a plausibility argument

  • Say \(\Delta M_{\rm ext} < 0 \)
  • Roughly, microscopic energy levels have shifted down
  • \(\Delta S\) is change at fixed \(M\)
  • Downward shift in energies \(\implies\) more ways of making state \(M\), \(\Delta S>0\)
  • Opposite for \(\Delta M_{\rm ext}>0\)

Technical Argument: Outline

Basic Idea: Treat EFT parameters \(\epsilon\) as additional thermo. variables

Microscopically: \(\mathcal{L}_{0}\longrightarrow \mathcal{L}_{0}+\epsilon\Delta\mathcal{L}\)
Thermodynamically: \((S,M,\Qvec)\longrightarrow (S,M,\Qvec,\epsilon)\)

Strategy: Characterize changes via \(\partial_{\epsilon} M_{\rm ext}(\Qvec,\epsilon)\) and \(\partial_{\epsilon} S(M,\Qvec,\epsilon)\)

In Practice: Use \(G=M-TS-\mvec\cdot\Qvec=G_{0}(\mvec,T)+\epsilon \Delta G(\mvec,T)\)

Find: \(\partial_{\epsilon} M\propto \partial_{\epsilon} S\propto \Delta G\)

LO calculation above is typical use case, but relation holds at all order in \(\epsilon\)
[Cremonini et al., 1912.11161]

Computing \(\partial_{\epsilon} M_{\rm ext}(\Qvec,\epsilon)\)

Entire Proof: Chain rule and thermodynamic identities repeatedly

\[ \require{enclose} \begin{align} \left(\frac{\partial M}{\partial \epsilon}\right)_{T,\Qvec}&=\left(\partial_{\epsilon}\left(G+TS+\mvec\cdot\Qvec\right)\right)_{T,\Qvec} =\Delta G(T,\mvec)+T\left(\frac{\partial S}{\partial \epsilon}\right)_{T,\Qvec} \end{align} \]

We are interested in \(\partial_{\epsilon}M_{\rm ext}(\Qvec,\epsilon)=\partial_{\epsilon}M(T,\Qvec,\epsilon)\Big|_{T=0}\)

Third Law Assumption: \(\lim _{T\to 0}S(T,\Qvec,\epsilon)={\rm const}\)

Final term vanishes above:

\[ \bbox[5px, border: 2px solid red]{\partial_{\epsilon}M_{\rm ext}(\Qvec,\epsilon)=\Delta G(T,\mvec)\Big|_{T=0}} \]

Computing \(\partial_{\epsilon} S(M,Q,\epsilon)\) [Reall and Santos, 1901.11535]

Essentially the same as \(\partial_{\epsilon}M_{\rm ext}\). Identities + Chain Rule

\[ \require{enclose} \begin{align} \beta \Delta G&=\left(\frac{\partial \beta G}{\partial\epsilon }\right)_{T,\mvec}=\beta\left(\frac{\partial M}{\partial \epsilon}\right)_{T,\mvec}-\beta\mvec\cdot\left(\frac{\partial \Qvec }{\partial \epsilon}\right)_{T,\mvec}-\left(\frac{\partial S}{\partial \epsilon}\right)_{T,\mvec}\nn &=-\left(\frac{\partial S}{\partial \epsilon}\right)_{M,\Qvec} \quad ({\rm Using \ Identities}) \end{align} \]

Combining: Exact Result

\[ \bbox[5px, border: 2px solid red]{\partial_{\epsilon}M_{\rm ext}(\Qvec,\epsilon)=\lim_{T\to 0}-T\left(\frac{\partial S}{\partial \epsilon}\right)_{M,\Qvec}} \]

Totally arbitrary \(\Qvec\), completely general.
Related approximate results hold away from extremality
