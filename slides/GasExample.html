Example

Basic Example: Ideal Gas

2D ideal gas w/ \(N\) particles: \(H=\sum_i \frac{p_i^2}{2m}\). Here, \(\Qvec=\{N,L\}\)

Basic Thermodynamic relations:

\[ M_0(N,T)=NT \quad S_0(N,T)\approx N\left(\ln (2\pi T m L^2)+2)\right) \]

Clearly, energy is bounded by zero

\[ M(N,T)>M_{\rm ext}^{0}(N)=0 \]

Basic Example: Ideal Gas \(\Delta M_{\rm ext}(N)\)

Curve the disk: \(H\longrightarrow H-\lambda\left(1-\frac{r^2}{L^2}\right)\)

Particles sit at bottom of well at \( T=0\)

Minimium energy is shifted by \(-N\lambda\)

\[ M(N,T)> M_{\rm ext}^{0}(N)+\Delta M_{\rm ext}(N)\quad \bbox[5px, border: 2px solid red]{\Delta M_{\rm ext}(N)=-N\lambda} \]

Ideal Gas \(\Delta S(M,N)\)

Consider a state \((M,N)\) which is slightly perturbed, \(\Delta T\ll T_0\). Find:

\[ T(M,N)=\frac{M}{N}\left(1+\frac{N\lambda}{2M}+\ldots\right)\quad \Delta S(M,N)=\frac{N^2\lambda}{2M}\left(1-\frac{N\lambda}{6}+\ldots\right) \]

Inspecting above and \(\Delta M_{\rm ext}(N)=-N\lambda\):

\[ \bbox[5px, border: 2px solid red]{\Delta M_{\rm ext}(N)=-T(M,N)\Delta S(M,N)\left(1-\frac{\Delta T}{T_0}+\ldots\right)} \]

I will argue that such results are generic: \(\Delta M_{\rm ext}\approx -T\Delta S \)
